Lindenlea Community Centre

Lindenlea Community Centre 

is a non-profit recreational center serving the residents of the Lindenlea, New Edinburgh, Rockcliffe Park, Beachwood and Vanier areas.
The LCC offers many activities both indoors and outdoors. There are classes and courses ranging from arts and crafts to fitness and well being. There is something for everyone.

One of the most successful and longest running programs offered at the center is World Taekwondo taught by Master Taylor Haas. In 1993 Master Taylor Haas established the first Taekwondo class at the Lindenlea Community Center in affiliation with Grand Master Tae Eun Lee (9th Dan) Moo Kwang Taekwondo school.

Grandmaster Lee is a native of South Korea. In 1977 Grandmaster Lee opened his first school in Ottawa. He is the only Korean Grandmaster in Ottawa and is one of the major promoters of the art and sport of Taekwondo in Canada.

 

Grandmaster Lee developed the Moo Kwang philosophy, reflecting his belief that Taekwondo can unite the mind, body, and spirit. The Philosophy of Moo Kwang Taekwondo begins with the name of the martial art itself.

The interpretation of the words are: Moo is non-stop and eternal. Kwang  is brightness. Moo Kwang emphasizes focus on three main aspects of your life. By giving each the required energy you will lead a successful life.

For almost 30 years, Master Taylor Haas has had hundreds of students ranging from 5 to 70 years old. People come from all over the Ottawa area to learn and train at LCC TKD. 

Master Haas has taught students up to the ranks of 1st, 2nd, and 3rd Dan black belt. All black belt promotions are conducted by Korean Grandmaster Tae Eun Lee (WT 9th Dan). Lindenlea Taekwondo black belts are certified with the Kukkiwon, the head quarters of World Taekwondo in Seoul Korea.

Our Instructors

Master Taylor Haas

Master Taylor Haas is currently a 4th Dan black belt. He is the founder and head instructor of the Lindenlea Taekwondo. He joined the Tae Eun Lee Ottawa Taekwondo School in 1984. He learned the Chang Moo Kwon and Chung Myung Kwon systems of Taekwondo directly by Grandmaster Lee himself.
Over the years he competed in sparring at the local, provincial, and national levels. He won silver in the 1990 provincials and won gold at the Canadian Tae E. Lee Cup in advanced black belt poomse (patterns).
Master Haas was one of the original instructors to teach women’s self defense / Taekwondo at the University of Ottawa.
Master Haas has devoted a large part of his life to teaching the Moo Kwang Taekwondo system to countless numbers of students. He teaches with enthusiasm and passion which makes it a fun and structured environment ideal for students of all ages, regardless of their individual abilities or disabilities.
Master Haas believes the Moo Kwang philosophy isn’t only for the art and sport of Taekwondo, but in fact, it truly is a way of life.

Alexandra Vincent

Alexandra Vincent currently holds a 3rd Dan black belt and has been studying and training Moo Kwang Taekwondo for over 20 years. 


After Mrs. Vincent received her 2nd Dan black belt at LCC TKD, she joined the main headquarters with Grandmaster Tae Eun Lee (9th Dan). Her knowledge and skills in Moo Kwan Taekwondo flourished under the direct instruction of Grandmaster Lee. She became an instructor and has taught at Lindenlea for over 12 years. 


Her understanding of the complexity of executing techniques makes her a highly respected instructor at Lindenlea Taekwondo.
